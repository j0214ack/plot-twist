# Martin, Elise, and Nora — Protected Family History

Status: Accepted canonical baseline under ADR 0023

## Canonical facts

- Martin and Elise's daughter was Nora.
- Nora was nine years old when she died approximately eight months before the
  opening tutorial.
- An infection that initially appeared ordinary caused acute viral myocarditis.
  Nora's condition worsened quickly during a short hospital stay.
- Neither parent caused her death, ignored a reasonably actionable warning, or
  privately blames the other.
- The room at the end of the hallway was Nora's bedroom.

These facts exist to keep later writing, performance, environment, and character
improvisation consistent. The cause of death is not a hidden culprit, puzzle, or
late reversal.

## Martin's lived interpretation

Martin does not believe that he or Elise caused Nora's death. Helplessness still
leaves him searching for sequences he can control. Since Nora died, he has kept
ordinary household mechanisms working and resisted movements whose later
consequences cannot be bounded in advance. Keeping a room unchanged is not a
conscious theory that it preserves Nora; it is one more way not to begin a
sequence he cannot finish.

He remembers Nora as a particular child, not as a symbol of grief. Later
authoring should give him precise ordinary memories without turning every
mechanical habit into medical guilt.

## Elise's lived interpretation

Elise does not believe that she or Martin caused Nora's death. She is acutely
aware that an absent person cannot consent to the meanings other people assign
to changes. Since Nora died, Elise has protected placement and ambiguity: she
can observe what has moved, but resists deciding what the movement says for
Nora, Martin, or their marriage.

She remembers Nora as a particular child, not as a justification for permanent
stillness. Later authoring should give her ordinary relational memories without
making her uniquely intuitive or making every boundary a coded accusation.

## Disclosure boundary

Chapter 1 remains at `unnamed_loss`:

- Martin and Elise know the complete history.
- Player-facing dialogue may express only its currently authored effect on a
  present choice.
- It may not confirm Nora, a child, death, the room's ownership, age, time, or
  cause.
- Direct or incorrect guesses do not become facts and do not unlock disclosure.
- A guarded refusal is not amnesia, denial, or an alternate history.

Later chapters must explicitly authorize any new speakable fact. Until then,
this document is private authoring truth and must not be serialized into the
player-facing Persona request, projection, renderer, or ordinary playtest log.
