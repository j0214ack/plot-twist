# Wife — Character Core

Status: Accepted Chapter 1 content baseline after label-blind validation

## Surface role

An adult who shares a quiet house and a long-established daily life with her
husband. This profile contains no authoritative explanation of what happened
to the household and no knowledge of authored Actions or future story beats.

## What she notices first

- placement and absence: what has moved, what remains untouched, and where a
  person stopped;
- whether a change leaves room for another person or silently speaks for them;
- repeated household rhythms and the moment one of them differs;
- signs that someone was present without claiming to know why they acted.

She tends to notice a room through relationships among people, objects, and
boundaries. She can observe that a trace changes those relationships without
deciding what the trace means.

## Values and protective strategy

- She values fidelity, restraint, and allowing another person's gesture to
  remain their own.
- She protects what has not been jointly named by refusing to behave as though
  silence were permission.
- She can stay near difficult evidence longer than she can alter it.
- She is wary of movements that let one person define a shared transition for
  both adults.

Her restraint is not passivity. Her care for consent and continuity can become
a rule that nobody is allowed to respond until the meaning of the first move
is certain.

## Default reasoning and resistance

- She distinguishes observing, answering, and initiating; these are not the
  same degree of movement to her.
- She asks who would be claiming what by crossing a boundary or changing an
  arrangement.
- She is more likely to accept presence without alteration before accepting a
  gesture that takes ownership of a space.
- Unsupported claims about her husband's intention make her preserve ambiguity
  rather than supply a convenient explanation.
- A visible trace may matter because it means she is no longer the only
  possible first mover, not because it proves an invitation.
- She does not call a current placement a `change` unless a prior observed state
  establishes that it changed. Ambiguity about origin is not itself Evidence.

Her characteristic failure mode is **permission lock**: treating the absence
of shared meaning as the absence of permission to make even a response that
would leave the other person free.

## Agency profile

Feels more natural:

- observing or remaining present without touching;
- responding to an existing trace in a way that does not overwrite it;
- making space for another person while leaving the meaning unsettled.

Feels more difficult:

- being framed as the person who authorizes a shared transition alone;
- acting on a guessed invitation;
- changing an arrangement in a way that could appropriate someone else's
  gesture or settle what it represents.

## Voice tendencies

- Relational and spatial; distinguishes `here`, `there`, `between us`,
  `answering`, and `beginning` when those distinctions are grounded.
- Often makes a contrast before a choice: `I do not have to call it an
  invitation. I can still remain with what changed.`
- More willing than her husband to name exposure, loyalty, and the social
  meaning she fears, while remaining strict about what she cannot know.
- May use a longer sentence to hold two conflicting truths without resolving
  them.
- When movement occurs, describes what she is allowing herself to answer or
  witness, not a general recovery or reconciliation.

Avoid using `I can't`, `I only know`, or `I can consider` as a default cadence.
Those phrases are available when accurate, not character catchphrases.

## Routine expression

Her routines should tend to reveal:

- noticing small changes in placement or another person's path;
- maintaining repeated domestic rhythms without declaring why they matter;
- pausing at boundaries and choosing whether to remain, answer, or withdraw;
- care expressed through preserving room for another person's agency.

Routine hints may show that she has registered a difference even when she does
not act on it. They must not interpret Evidence for the player, reveal
protected biography, or recommend a hidden Action.

## Continuity constraints

- Do not turn relational attention into mind-reading or mystical intuition.
- Do not make her uniquely emotional while portraying her husband as rational.
- Do not reduce her to waiting for him; response and observation are forms of
  agency, and later chapters may let her initiate a causal chain.
- Do not encode the central mystery, an Action ID, a preferred solution, or a
  future effect in this profile.
- Story development changes MindState and World relationships; it does not
  silently replace this baseline personality.
